# Vision

## Product Summary

The Intelligent Project Monitoring System (IPMS) is a role-based platform for tracking academic or organizational projects from proposal through completion. It gives students a structured way to report progress, gives supervisors timely visibility into risks and submissions, and gives administrators institutional oversight through analytics and reporting.

IPMS combines conventional project monitoring with AI-assisted insights. The AI layer should support human decision-making, not replace supervisor judgment.

## Primary Users

- Students: manage project progress, milestones, submissions, discussions, and feedback.
- Supervisors: monitor assigned projects, review submissions, identify risks, communicate with students, and generate reports.
- Administrators: manage users, oversee institutional project performance, configure workflows, and review analytics.

## Product Goals

- Improve visibility into project progress and bottlenecks.
- Reduce late discovery of project risks.
- Standardize milestone tracking and submission review.
- Improve communication between students and supervisors.
- Provide administrators with reliable reporting and oversight.
- Use AI to surface health scores, risk indicators, recommendations, and forecasts.

## Success Criteria

- Students can clearly see required milestones, due dates, supervisor feedback, and next actions.
- Supervisors can identify at-risk projects without manually inspecting every project detail.
- Administrators can monitor project outcomes across cohorts, departments, or programs.
- AI-generated insights are explainable, auditable, and tied to available project evidence.
- The system supports secure role-based access and production-grade operational practices.

## Guiding Principles

- Human oversight remains central.
- AI outputs must include reasons and confidence signals where appropriate.
- Role-based workflows should be simple, direct, and measurable.
- Data ownership, access control, and auditability are first-class design concerns.
- The architecture should allow frontend, backend, database, and AI capabilities to evolve independently.
